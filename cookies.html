What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your actions and preferences (such as login, language, region) over a period of time, so you don’t have to keep re-entering them whenever you come back.

Cookies can also be used to track site usage analytics, improve performance, and deliver personalized advertising.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (e.g., language or region preferences) to provide enhanced and personalized features.

Advertising and Targeting Cookies

Understanding Different Browser Settings for Cookies

Your browser settings determine how cookies are managed on your device. Most modern browsers provide options to block third-party cookies, delete existing cookies, or prevent all cookies. Below are specifics for popular browsers:

Google Chrome

  • Navigate to Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data.
  • Choose to allow all cookies, block third-party cookies, or block all cookies.
  • Manage exceptions by allowing or blocking cookies for specific sites.

Mozilla Firefox

  • Open Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data.
  • Set Enhanced Tracking Protection to standard or strict.
  • Manually clear cookies and site data or set exceptions.

Safari

  • Go to Preferences > Privacy.
  • Enable "Prevent cross-site tracking" to limit third-party cookies.
  • Manage website data for clearing cookies from specific domains.

Microsoft Edge

  • Access Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies and site data.
  • Choose cookie blocking preferences and add site exceptions.

Technical Overview of Cookies

Cookies are text files containing small pieces of data, such as a unique identifier. When you visit a website, the server sends this file to your browser, which stores it locally and sends it back with subsequent requests to that site. This process allows sites to remember your session and preferences efficiently.

Types of cookies include session cookies, which expire when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain until they expire or are deleted.

Secure and HttpOnly cookies add layers of protection by limiting access to cookie data and restricting transmission to secure HTTPS connections.
